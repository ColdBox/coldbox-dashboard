The above method is the default one.The dashboard requires that ColdBox be installed in the default location of thewebroot. However, if you have refactored it to your custom needs, you will needto alter two settings:1) index.cfm - Alter the location of the coldbox.cfm file2) config.xml.cfm - Alter the coldbox_location setting to point to the coldbox                    system folder. DO NOT APPEND A / AT THE END.                    Example: /frameworks/coldbox_2 is the new location1) <cfinclude template="/frameworks/coldbox_2/coldbox/system/coldbox.cfm">2) <Setting name="Coldbox_Location" 		value="/frameworks/coldbox_2/coldbox/system"/>You can also alter any of the settings on the config.xml.cfm to meet your needssuch as:1) OwnerEmail2) BugTracerReports3) MailServerSettings4) ReinitPassword5) DebugPasswordThen reach it by going tohttp://localhost/coldbox/dashboardThe default password is coldbox********************************************************************************SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS********************************************************************************- ColdFusion MX 6.X and above- BlueDragon 6.X and above- Railo 2.0 and above- Application Scope for the Frameworks internal structures- Session Scope for messaging.********************************************************************************CHANGELOG********************************************************************************Version 2.0.0- Update to the 2.0 ColdBox framework- Moved to JQueryVersion 2.1.0- #1 Domain Model refactoring- #2 Addition of Framework Conventions- #3 Application GeneratorVersion 2.2.0  ********************************************************************************THE DAILY BREAD********************************************************************************"I am the way, and the truth, and the life; no one comes to the Father, but by me (JESUS)"Jn 14:1-12"Anyone who drinks the water I give will never thirstâ€” not ever.
